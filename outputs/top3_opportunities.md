# Top 3 Strategic Cost-Saving Opportunities

_Analysis based on 386 vendors and $7,887,359 total annual spend_

---

## Opportunity 1: Salesforce CRM License Audit & Contract Renegotiation

**Estimated Annual Savings: $500,000**

Salesforce UK Ltd accounts for $3,117,226 — nearly 40% of the company's entire vendor spend — making it by far the highest-leverage renegotiation target in the portfolio. At this spend level, a structured license audit typically uncovers 20–30% of seats that are underutilized, inactive, or assigned to churned employees. Using the audit findings as leverage, the company is well-positioned to renegotiate terms against competitive alternatives such as Microsoft Dynamics and HubSpot. A conservative 15% reduction on the Salesforce contract alone yields $468K in savings. Additionally, HubSpot Ireland ($32,187) overlaps materially with Salesforce's native marketing automation capabilities and should be consolidated, removing the license cost entirely. This opportunity is highly actionable: contract renegotiations of this type are routine at renewal cycles and do not require any operational disruption.

**Vendors involved:** Salesforce UK Ltd-UK ($3,117,226), HubSpot Ireland Limited ($32,187)

## Opportunity 2: Croatia & Global Facilities Vendor Consolidation

**Estimated Annual Savings: $245,000**

The Facilities department is the most fragmented in the portfolio: 122 vendors consuming $1,216,417 — an average of under $10K per vendor. The most impactful single action is consolidating the two overlapping Zagreb office leases: Zagrebtower D.O.O. ($183,754) and Weking D.O.O. ($144,093) serve the same function in the same city, and exiting the smaller location is estimated to eliminate approximately $130,000 in annual lease obligations. Beyond that, the vendor tail is extensive — more than 40 Croatian vendors fall below $5,000 in annual spend (catering, parking, cleaning, entertainment, courier services), collectively accounting for roughly $60,000 that can be terminated or absorbed into primary service contracts. A further $35,000 can be recovered by consolidating overlapping real estate advisors (JLL and CBRE), merging Croatian catering vendors into one supplier, and terminating one-time event/hospitality vendors being tracked as recurring lines.

**Vendors involved:** Zagrebtower D.O.O. ($183,754), Weking D.O.O. ($144,093), Jones Lang Lasalle NSW ($16,274), CBRE Limited ($8,395), and 40+ sub-$5K Croatian tail vendors (~$60,000 combined)

## Opportunity 3: Finance & Audit Advisory Rationalization

**Estimated Annual Savings: $160,000**

The Finance department runs $750,708 across 25 vendors, with significant duplication among audit, tax, and advisory firms. BDO LLP ($343,081) already serves as the primary auditor, yet Grant Thornton ($46,539) and Harmonic Group ($65,418) are both flagged for consolidation due to overlapping audit and financial advisory mandates — eliminating these two vendors frees ~$112K annually. A duplicative FP&A platform situation also exists: Sage UK ($46,895) and Planful ($27,741) both serve financial planning and management functions; retaining Planful (the cloud-native, more strategically aligned platform) and migrating off Sage yields a further ~$35K. Terminating Cedar Recruitment ($18,537) — a finance staffing agency whose volume can be absorbed by the internal TA function — rounds out the savings. Combined, these actions reduce Finance vendor count by at least six, simplify the audit governance structure, and eliminate redundant software licensing.

**Vendors involved:** Grant Thornton ($46,539), Harmonic Group Limited ($65,418), Sage UK Limited ($46,895), Cedar Recruitment Ltd ($18,537), Crowe Horwath Revizija ($4,062)
